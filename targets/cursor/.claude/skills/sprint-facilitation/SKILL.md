# Sprint facilitation — ceremonies that earn their time

## Sprint planning
- Start from a **sprint goal** — one sentence of intent, not just a pile of tickets. It's what you'd demo.
- Select a **capacity-fit** backlog from *Ready* items only (Definition of Ready) — fit to measured capacity, not hope. Flag what's deferred and why.
- Order by value + dependency; identify the first pull-able item.

## Running the sprint
- **Limit WIP** — finish before you start; a board full of in-progress is slower, not faster.
- Surface blockers daily and act on them; the standup is for impediments, not status theatre.
- Protect the goal: new mid-sprint work is negotiated against it, not silently absorbed (scope creep).

## Blameless retrospective
- **Blameless** — the system, not the person; people act rationally given their constraints, so fix the constraints.
- Ground it in **sprint data** (velocity, carryover, defects, cycle time), not vibes.
- Output **1–3 owned, tracked action items** with a name and a due sprint — a retro with 12 actions changes nothing; one that's done changes the next sprint. Track last retro's actions first.

## Status reporting
- Ground the RAG rating in **metrics + the RAID log**, not optimism. Red is information, not failure.
- Beware **watermelon status** — green outside, red inside; if the metrics and the rating disagree, trust the metrics.
- Tailor to the audience (exec vs team) but never change the underlying truth between them.

## Smells
- A sprint with no goal → a to-do list, not a sprint.
- Overcommitting every sprint → capacity ignored; carryover hides the real velocity.
- Retro actions with no owner/date → nothing changes; it becomes a venting session.
- A green status the metrics don't support → watermelon; the surprise lands at the worst time.
